Introduction

LLMs like GPT are often used for practical tasks—summarizing documents, writing code, even crafting apologies. But what happens when we ask them to dream?

For this post, I asked GPT to invent a future society from scratch—a world shaped by a single core change. The goal: to test how well it could simulate system-level thinking across culture, economy, politics, and human emotion. Can GPT not only generate a sci-fi world, but make it feel real?

This experiment touches on several big questions: - Can LLMs simulate complex cause-effect reasoning across social systems? - Can they infer plausible consequences from a single speculative premise? - Do they exhibit creativity that feels “emergent” or human-like?

Prompt:

“Invent a future society where sleep is no longer biologically necessary. Describe what daily life, work, relationships, and culture look like.”

GPT’s Response (excerpted):

In the world of Everwake, the need for sleep was eliminated in 2084 through widespread neuro-modification. Within a generation, human society reshaped itself around continuous wakefulness.

Daily Life: With no circadian rhythm, people live in modular, shift-based communities. Cities never “sleep,” but hum with 24/7 activity. Time is sliced differently—days are no longer 24 hours, but broken into personal cycles of productivity, leisure, and reflection.

Work: Most people work shorter but more frequent shifts. Jobs that once demanded long hours (medicine, transport, customer service) now operate with seamlessly rotating crews. Creativity-based industries thrive, with new norms around “mental cooldown” rituals.

Relationships: Romantic partnerships are often scheduled around overlapping activity cycles. Some couples sync “rest hours” to enjoy downtime together, even if no sleep is involved.

Culture: Art and storytelling now explore the “myth of sleep.” Dreamlike films and music are created to simulate the lost experience. A new subculture, “Slumberpunk,” romantically re-enacts bedtime rituals with aestheticized nostalgia.

Meanwhile, others resist. A growing movement of “Sleep Revivalists” believe rest is more than biology—it’s a form of spiritual reset. They practice voluntary hibernation, hoping to reclaim what tech erased.

Notes & Analysis

GPT’s response didn’t just list sci-fi clichés—it constructed a coherent sociotechnical system. Here’s what stood out:

1. Emergent Systems Thinking

  • The model connected biological change to cascading effects on urban design, timekeeping, labor models, and intimacy rituals.
  • It even predicted countercultural backlash—a hallmark of nuanced sociopolitical modeling. The “Sleep Revivalists” were especially evocative, suggesting that GPT can simulate ideological opposition within a world it just created.

2. Emotional Worldbuilding

  • The “Slumberpunk” aesthetic and bedtime nostalgia aren’t just cool ideas—they show that GPT can simulate emotional reactions to systemic change, including grief, longing, and romanticism.
  • This gets at one of our course’s core questions: Do LLMs understand emotion? Maybe not. But they can simulate how humans would feel under specific conditions—with surprising fluency.

3. Temporal Imagination

  • GPT didn’t just describe a future—it designed temporal rhythms. Breaking the 24-hour cycle is a subtle but powerful sign of imaginative reasoning. It reflects modeling of time as a social construct, not a fixed input.

Follow-Up Prompt:

“What kind of music would exist in this society?”

Music in Everwake often features asynchronous rhythms and polyrhythmic layering, reflecting the constant flux of overlapping time cycles.

Genres like “Circadian Jazz” and “REMcore” blend ambient tones with artificial glitches to evoke the lost feeling of drifting into sleep.

Some artists use AI to generate sonic hallucinations—personalized dreamscapes streamed during meditation breaks.

Live performances are held at liminal hours—3:17 AM or 11:02 PM—challenging traditional “primetime” and inviting listeners to find their own sense of night.

Notes:

This response shows GPT extending the world’s logic into aesthetic dimensions. It adapts music to societal conditions, modeling not only technological change but its impact on artistic expression.

Conclusion

This worldbuilding experiment highlights GPT’s ability to: - Simulate multi-layered systems from a single speculative change - Generate emotional, cultural, and aesthetic consequences of those systems - Reflect a kind of “dream logic” that—while not sentient—is uncannily creative

While GPT doesn’t “imagine” like humans, it stitches together enough patterns, emotional motifs, and systemic logic to create the illusion of imagination. And sometimes, that’s all you need to build a world you want to explore.
