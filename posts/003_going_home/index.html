A Trip Home

Can GPT plan a 3 day itinerary to explore my hometown?
LLMs
prompting
logic
Author

Jayme Chen

Published

February 25, 2025

Introduction

I’m feeling a bit homesick today, so I asked GPT to create a 3-day travel itenerary for my hometown. I know the area well, so I know the great beaches and restaurants to go to. I have a personal itenerary for when I get home, so I’m interested to see if GPT’s plans will align with mine.

Lets Go!

Prompt 1

Prompt: I’m going on a 3 day trip to Orange County, California

Response: That sounds like a fun trip! Are you looking for recommendations on vegan food, activities, or anything specific while you’re there?

Notes

  • Interestingly, rather than immediately offering me a travel plan, GPT agreed that my trip sounded fun and inquired if I wanted any specific information
  • This is more similar to a response I would expect from a human. Upon hearing about my trip, I would not expect a friend to launch into a travel itenerary, so GPT modeled a normal conversation quite well

  • However, it anticipates that I will ask it to retreive some information, likely because there is not much reason for me to arbitrarily inform an AI model of my travel plans

Prompt 2

Prompt: Create a comprehensive travel plan for me

Response:

Here’s a 3-day travel plan for your trip to Orange County, California, with a mix of great food, scenic spots, and fun activities.

Day 1: Arrival & Exploring Costa Mesa + Newport Beach

Morning

  • Arrive in Orange County (John Wayne Airport - SNA is the most convenient).
  • Breakfast at Rooster Café (Costa Mesa) – Famous for breakfast burritos and chilaquiles.
  • Visit South Coast Plaza – One of the largest luxury malls in the U.S., great for shopping or strolling.

Afternoon

  • Lunch at The Crack Shack (Costa Mesa) – Known for amazing fried chicken sandwiches.
  • Head to Newport Beach – Walk along Balboa Peninsula, explore the Fun Zone, or take a Duffy boat ride through the harbor.
  • Relax at Crystal Cove State Park – A scenic beach with hiking trails and tide pools.

Evening

  • Sunset at Laguna Beach – Stop by Heisler Park for breathtaking ocean views.
  • Dinner at The Deck on Laguna Beach – A beachfront restaurant with fresh seafood, steaks, and great cocktails.
  • Optional: Rooftop drinks at Mozambique – A South African-inspired bar with live music and ocean views.

Day 2: Adventure & Nature in Anaheim + Huntington Beach

Morning

  • Breakfast at Snooze, an A.M. Eatery (Tustin) – A great spot for pancakes, eggs Benedict, and brunch cocktails.
  • Explore Disneyland or Disney California Adventure (optional if you’re interested in theme parks) – Try the famous Dole Whip, churros, and unique themed food throughout the parks.

Afternoon

  • Lunch at The Blind Rabbit (Anaheim Packing District) – A hidden speakeasy-style restaurant with great food and drinks.
  • Hike at Peter’s Canyon Regional Park – A moderate hike with excellent views of Orange County.
  • Visit Huntington Beach – Walk down the famous pier, check out Pacific City, or rent bikes along the boardwalk.

Evening

  • Dinner at Bear Flag Fish Co. (Newport Beach) – Fresh seafood, poke bowls, and sushi.
  • Bonfire at Bolsa Chica State Beach – Experience a classic California beach bonfire (bring marshmallows for s’mores!).

Day 3: Irvine + Departure

Morning

  • Brunch at Haven Craft Kitchen + Bar (Orange) – A great spot for chicken and waffles, burgers, and brunch cocktails.
  • Explore the Orange County Great Park (Irvine) – Ride the Great Park Balloon for a panoramic view.
  • Visit Tanaka Farms – A family-run farm offering tours, fresh fruit picking, and a farm stand with fresh produce.

Afternoon

  • Lunch at In-N-Out Burger (try it “Animal Style”!)
  • Visit The LAB Anti-Mall (Costa Mesa) – A trendy, artsy shopping area with unique stores and photo-worthy spots.
  • Head to the Airport (SNA or LAX)

Other Optional Activities

  • Whale Watching Tour (Newport Beach or Dana Point)
  • Shopping at Irvine Spectrum Center
  • Visit Mission San Juan Capistrano (for history lovers)
  • Check out Anaheim Packing District (for food and drinks)

Would you like any adjustments based on your interests?

Notes

  • After I explicitly asked it for a travel plan, GPT generated a comprehensive list of plans for me to engage in
  • The plan is split into 3 days, and each day is split into morning, afternoon and evening. This a very inttuitive design and is likely the format I would use to create a travel plan as well.
  • The restaurants that GPT mentioned are quite well-known in my area, and the activities explore common tourist attractions.
  • The days are also planned in a way that each day explores a new area of Orange County, which is very smart and useful, as in my statement I implied that I wanted to see all of Orange County.

Prompt 3

I’m staying in Aliso Viejo

Response:

Since you’re staying in Aliso Viejo, I’ll adjust the itinerary to make it more convenient for you while still keeping the best of Orange County.

Day 1: Arrival & Exploring Aliso Viejo + Laguna Beach

Morning

  • Arrive in Orange County (John Wayne Airport - SNA is the closest).
  • Breakfast at Plumeria Café by Stacks (Aliso Viejo) – Known for delicious pancakes, omelets, and Hawaiian-style breakfast.
  • Relax at Aliso & Wood Canyons Wilderness Park – A great local spot for hiking or scenic views.

Afternoon

  • Lunch at The Stand Natural Foods (Laguna Beach) – A casual spot with great sandwiches, salads, and smoothies.
  • Explore Laguna Beach – Walk around Heisler Park, visit Main Beach, and check out local art galleries.
  • Stop by Thousand Steps Beach – One of the most beautiful hidden beaches in OC.

Evening

  • Sunset dinner at The Deck on Laguna Beach – Oceanfront dining with seafood, steaks, and cocktails.
  • Optional: Drinks at The Rooftop Lounge – A great spot for sunset views and craft cocktails.

Day 2: Adventure & Huntington Beach

Morning

  • Breakfast at Snooze, an A.M. Eatery (Tustin) – Famous for pancakes, eggs Benedict, and breakfast cocktails.
  • Hike at Top of the World (Laguna Beach) – A must-see viewpoint with stunning panoramic views.

Afternoon

  • Lunch at Bear Flag Fish Co. (Newport Beach) – Fresh poke bowls, seafood tacos, and sushi.
  • Visit Huntington Beach – Walk along the pier, rent a bike on the boardwalk, or check out Pacific City for shopping.

Evening

  • Dinner at Duke’s Huntington Beach – Hawaiian-inspired seafood and steaks with ocean views.
  • Bonfire at Bolsa Chica State Beach – Classic SoCal experience with s’mores!

Day 3: Aliso Viejo + Departure

Morning

  • Brunch at Café Anastasia (Laguna Beach) – Cozy spot with great coffee, French toast, and eggs.
  • Visit Soka University & Peace Lake – A beautiful hidden gem in Aliso Viejo for a peaceful morning walk.

Afternoon

  • Lunch at In-N-Out Burger (Mission Viejo) – A must-try for a California classic.
  • Explore Irvine Spectrum Center – A great shopping area with entertainment and dining before heading to the airport.
  • Departure from SNA or LAX

Other Optional Activities Nearby

  • Dana Point Whale Watching
  • Mission San Juan Capistrano
  • Shopping at The Shops at Mission Viejo
  • Stand-up paddleboarding at Dana Point Harbor

This keeps your activities closer to Aliso Viejo while still giving you a great mix of beaches, food, and fun. Let me know if you’d like any tweaks!

Notes

  • To really test GPT’s knowledge, I told it that I am staying in my exact hometown, a small suburb that is not very well-known. I wanted to see if it still had knowledge of the local restaurants and activities.
  • GPT blew me away with its knowledge, starting off day one with breakfast at my favorite cafe by my house. The first day is also dedicated to exploring Laguna Beach, my favorite beach.
  • Days 2 and 3 explore other nearby beaches, featuring restaurant recommendations that hold a special place in my heart as well as some that are new to me.

Prompt 4

Prompt: Refine the Aliso Viejo travel plan to fit me, I’m Asian

Response:

Here’s a refined 3-day travel plan for your Aliso Viejo trip, incorporating Asian food and cultural preferences while keeping a balance of scenic, fun, and relaxing activities.

Day 1: Arrival & Exploring Aliso Viejo + Laguna Beach

Morning

  • Arrive in Orange County (John Wayne Airport - SNA is the closest).
  • Breakfast at Stacks Pancake House (Mission Viejo) – A local favorite with Japanese-inspired Hawaiian breakfast options like Loco Moco and Spam Musubi.
  • Relax at Aliso & Wood Canyons Wilderness Park – A scenic nature reserve perfect for an easy walk or a light hike.

Afternoon

  • Lunch at Mokkoji Shabu Shabu (Mission Viejo) – A cozy Japanese hotpot spot with high-quality meats, seafood, and rich broths.
  • Explore Laguna Beach – Walk around Heisler Park, visit Main Beach, and check out local art galleries.
  • Visit Thousand Steps Beach – A hidden gem known for its crystal-clear water and tide pools.

Evening

  • Dinner at Starfish Laguna Beach – A modern Pan-Asian restaurant with flavorful dishes like Thai curries, sushi, and dumplings.
  • Dessert at Gelato Paradiso – A must-visit for rich, handcrafted Italian gelato.

Day 2: Asian Food, Shopping, & Huntington Beach

Morning

  • Breakfast at Tim Ho Wan (Irvine) – The famous Michelin-starred Hong Kong dim sum restaurant, known for BBQ pork buns and shrimp dumplings.
  • Hike at Top of the World (Laguna Beach) – One of the best panoramic views in Orange County.

Afternoon

  • Lunch at HiroNori Craft Ramen (Irvine) – One of the best ramen spots in California, offering Tonkotsu and Shoyu ramen with rich, creamy broth.
  • Shopping at Irvine Spectrum Center – A great spot for shopping with brands like Uniqlo and Muji, plus a giant Ferris wheel.
  • Drive to Huntington Beach – Walk along the Huntington Beach Pier, rent bikes on the boardwalk, or browse Pacific City for cool stores.

Evening

  • Dinner at Din Tai Fung (South Coast Plaza, Costa Mesa) – Legendary Taiwanese soup dumplings, noodles, and flavorful side dishes.
  • Bubble Tea at Omomo Tea Shoppe (Irvine) – One of the most popular boba shops in OC, known for its Sea Cream Jasmine Green Tea.
  • Optional: Bonfire at Bolsa Chica State Beach – A fun way to end the night with s’mores!

Day 3: Aliso Viejo + Departure

Morning

  • Brunch at J Zhou Oriental Cuisine (Tustin) – Upscale Cantonese dim sum with excellent seafood options.
  • Visit Soka University & Peace Lake – A hidden gem with beautiful Japanese-style gardens and a peaceful lake.

Afternoon

  • Lunch at In-N-Out Burger (Mission Viejo) – A California classic! Try it Animal Style for the full experience.
  • Last-minute shopping at The Shops at Mission Viejo – Great for souvenirs and fashion finds.
  • Departure from SNA or LAX

Other Optional Activities Nearby

  • Tanaka Farms (Irvine) – A Japanese-American family-run farm with fresh fruit picking.
  • Whale Watching in Dana Point – A top spot to see dolphins and migrating whales.
  • Visit Little Saigon (Westminster) – The largest Vietnamese community in the U.S., with amazing pho, bánh mì, and desserts.

This plan balances great Asian food, beautiful scenery, and local experiences while keeping it close to Aliso Viejo. Let me know if you’d like to tweak anything!

Notes

  • I was once again impressed by GPT’s knowledge of the area. It recommended many of my favorite Asian restaurants around my house.
  • GPT also kept a good amount of the activities the same, visiting the same beaches and hikes as to still explore the area well.
  • Even with the revisions, the plan keep the same amount of exploration and activity level as before, but with updated food suggestions featuring amazing Asian cuisine

Conclusion

Overall, I was extremely impressed by GPT’s knowledge of restaurants and activities in and around my hometown. Its suggestions featured many of my favorite cafes and beaches, and even introduced me to some new ones. GPT has proven to be an amazing trip planner, and I might just oncorporate some of its suggestions into my trip home for Spring Break!
